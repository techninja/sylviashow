First book should be out late July 2014, titled Super-Simple Arduino.</p> <p>Happening a <em>little</em> sooner, Sylvia (along with me, CraftNinja mom and little sister Terrific Talulah) will be in Washington D.C. this week for the very first White House Maker Faire, thanks in part to travel sponsor <a href="http://atmel.com">Atmel</a>'s unwavering dedication to education and the young Maker community, <em><strong>they rock!</strong></em></p> <p><a href="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/post-images/Dadmin/atmel_shirt.JPG" class="yoxview_thumbnail"><img class="post-img-c" typeof="foaf:Image" src="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/styles/blog_large/public/post-images/Dadmin/atmel_shirt.JPG?itok=hYoMyNGA" alt="Atmel Logo on a shirt" title="Atmel - Sylvia&#039;s travel sponsor for the White House Maker Faire" /></a></p> <p><a href="http://makezine.com/day-of-making/"><img alt="dayofmakinglogo.png" src="/sites/default/files/post-images/dayofmakinglogo.png" style="float:left; height:160px; margin-left:25px; margin-right:25px; width:160px" /></a></p> <p>For the majority of Makers wishing to take part in the festivities, don't worry! June 18th has been officially proclaimed as the <a href="http://makezine.com/day-of-making/">National Day of Making</a>. Get together with your local maker space, hacking group, or just your family, and get out there and make something! Post pictures and use the hash-tag  <a href="https://twitter.com/search?q=%23nationofmakers"><span style="color:#ff0000">#nationofmakers</span></a> to let the world know that you're doing your part and making something awesome <em>together</em>. There's tons of other suggestions on the Day of Making site, so go check it out!</p> <p>We'll be tweeting from the event live as long as we've got connectivity, so check <a href="https://twitter.com/makersylvia">@MakerSylvia</a> for updates before, during, and after for all the fun and surprises we're bound to find there.</p> <p>Good luck makers, young, old, and future. Lets all get out there and make something this June 18th and prove we are (even around the world) a <a href="https://twitter.com/search?q=%23nationofmakers"><span Robogames 2013, TWO Science Fairs, and an interview with Bill Nye and Levar Burton... in the same week?! <i><b>HOLY MOLY!!!</b></i></p> <p><a href="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/post-images/Dadmin/2013-04-21%2011.00.07.jpg" class="yoxview_thumbnail"><img class="post-img-r" typeof="foaf:Image" src="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/styles/blog_small/public/post-images/Dadmin/2013-04-21%2011.00.07.jpg?itok=WFLr2STV" width="200" height="200" alt="Sylvia posing with her custom made white cardboard box that reads &quot;Super-Awesome Sylvia - WATERCOLORBOT - Evil Mad Scientist&quot;" title="It&#039;s done!! Windell at EMSL helped with the logo and design, and the box fit EVERYTHING we needed, it was AWESOME! Loved the handle." /></a>Just a few weeks ago, I got a call from the White House staff, and they invited me to go to their 3rd ever science fair! Last year, Joey Hudy got chosen to go for making awesome kits, <i>and</i> got to show off his big orange marshmallow cannon. This year, MAKE magazine and Cognizant nominated me to come! The most insane part was that I only got one week warning, my school 6th grade science fair was on the same day as the white house one, <b><em>and</em></b> we’d have to leave on the last day of <a href="http://robogames.net">Robogames</a>!!! <strong><em>OH NOEsS!!</em></strong></p> <p>Luckily my dad helped organize and put together a fundraiser on <a href="http://www.gofundme.com/sylvia-science-fair">gofundme</a>, and we raised the full goal amount in less than a day thanks to all the awesome people who donated and helped get the word out! WOO hoo!</p> <p><a href="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/post-images/Dadmin/IMG_9266.JPG" class="yoxview_thumbnail"><img class="post-img-l" typeof="foaf:Image" src="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/styles/blog_small/public/post-images/Dadmin/IMG_9266.JPG?itok=z-joNLJ3" width="200" height="200" alt="Sylvia posing in her bicycle blouse at her hotel, ready to leave to the White House." title="Ready to go to the White House! Mom helped me curl my hair, and I got some new patches on my lab coat," /></a> On the Thursday before the Science Fair, I left to San Mateo to compete in Robogames 2013 with my WaterColorBot paint pen plotter (fill wrap-up post coming later). After an exciting couple days at Robogames (and winning a silver medal!), I boarded a plane to Washington DC, with my robot and project board all wrapped up in my awesome custom made carrying box. I was <strong><em>SOOOO</em></strong> excited!!</p> <p>We got to our hotel at 2am, slept ‘till 6am, got dressed all fancy, then left for the White House! (Not much sleep. :P)</p> <p><a href="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/post-images/Dadmin/IMG_9274.JPG" class="yoxview_thumbnail"><img class="post-img-r" typeof="foaf:Image" src="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/styles/blog_small/public/post-images/Dadmin/IMG_9274.JPG?itok=Hh-5vUp1" width="200" height="200" alt="Sylvia standing behind her science fair table at the White House, ready to display the WaterColorBot." title="At the White House, and I&#039;m ready! The letters above the board were painted with the bot, as were the other examples too." /></a>When we got there, I was checked by security and led to the room where I was supposed to set up in. I didn’t have much stuff to assemble, so I had a ton of time to figure out exactly how I would do my demo, and what the heck just happened. Craziness!!</p> <p>After about an hour, some people started to come by and I showed off my WaterColorBot. My demo worked with a Raspberry Pi as the computer, and connected to the iPad with a WIFI adapter that my dad help me make into an access point. After all of the other kids set up and looked around, we went outside and had some lunch and talked with lots of important people. After lunch settled down, I got interviewed by Levar Burton and Bill Nye!! Oh My Gosh!</p> <iframe style="margin: 0 auto; display: block;" width="640" height="360" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/GqSL4ciJqvg#t=1292s" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p> At 12:30, the science fair officially opened to the rest of the staff and VIP’s that got invited. I talked to a BUNCH of cool people and really had some fun talking with them all about how the bot works, how I came up with the design, and how I think it'll be perfect for girls my age to get into engineering with once we make it into a kit. I also talked lots and lots about other things I liked doing, plus all about my show.</p> <p><a href="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/post-images/Dadmin/IMG_9312.JPG" class="yoxview_thumbnail"><img class="post-img-c" typeof="foaf:Image" src="http://sylviashow.com/sites/default/files/styles/blog_large/public/post-images/Dadmin/IMG_9312.JPG?itok=y26sSLar" alt="Sylvia showing Nate Ball, host of PBS Kids Design Squad, and a group of onlookers how her WaterColorBot works" title="Showing Nate from PBS Kids Design Squad and all the cool VIPs how it works, and that it&#039;s cool!" /></a></p> <p>I hadn't had much sleep, but even though I'd been standing and talking for hours, I was totally pumped. It's hard to describe, but getting up there and talking about something awesome that you know pretty well, that people really want to hear, and have interesting questions about, is a lot of fun, and you wouldn't think so. But it is.... and I knew I had to hold out for the POTUS!</p> <iframe style="margin: 0 auto; display: block;" width="640" height="360" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/2HfgGDOZPCQ#t=1910s" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe><p> When it was time for the president to come in, all the regular people were moved into the entry hall. He then came in, said hello and started talking to the first booth. After a few teams/groups had talked with the president, I was next! He walked up, said hello and shook my hand. We talked, and then he held up my watercolorbot painted picture of the white house logo the press, and lots of camera shutters went off. He then talked with me a bit and drew something on the iPad for the bot to draw, “GO STEM!”. At the end he shook my hand again!!
